Where Did Jess Travel?

What's this page about?

On this page I will go through some of the countries that I visited.I am going to highlight activities, food reccomendations, and where we stayed. Lastly, I will go over tips for each location. I also included lots of pictures! As pictures speak a thousand words!

Switzerland

Interlaken

Description: An absolute beautiful town in Switzerland that makes you feel like you are in a Christmas village. A lot of outdoor activities and chocolate focused festivities. It is surrounded by a beautiful blue lake.

Activities

  • Chocolate making class: we went through the company Funky Chocolate
  • Kayaking: Hightide Kayak School
  • Skiing: They have ski rentals in town, and you can take a train to the mountain. It is called the Jungfrau region. Warning: you need to be an experienced skier it is not easy.
  • Paragliding & Skydiving: I didn’t do this, but Interlaken is known for these activities so do your research.

Food

  • They aren’t really known for good food here unless you want to spend a lot of money.
  • Funky chocolate- we ate here after out chocolate making class.
  • Just walk around and find cute little coffee/chocolate shops to eat at.

Where we stayed/how we got there:

  • Backpackers Villa Sonnehof Hostel: It was very clean, good location, and a good price.
  • Bus2Alps: We went through this company which picked us up in Florence and bussed us to Interlaken. It was a long bus ride but not too bad and less expensive.

Tips:

  • Book ahead! This is a very popular destination for students, so activities fill up fast so book things before you come.
  • It is very expensive! Try new things but really prioritize what you want to do as the activities are expensive.
  • If you are in Florence, do Bus2Alps company. They made the whole experience very seamless as they guided you through skiing and other activities.
  • If you are to ski, make sure you have a buddy and have skied before. It is challenging so be aware of that. If you can’t ski, you can still go and enjoy the mountains.

Zurich

Description: Another beautiful city in Switzerland. I went here in May and everything was so green and crisp. Zurich is more like a real city with a lot of shopping, restaurants, tall buildings, etc.

Activities

  • Shopping and exploring
  • Boat ride around the lake next to Zurich
  • Day trip to Lucerne.

Food

  • Swiss Chuchi: amazing fondue
  • Zeughauskeller: classic Swiss beer hall
  • Roots: Healthy lunch spot

Where we stayed/how we got there:

  • Stayed in the Marriot hotel.
  • Flew by plane to the Zurich airport.

Tips:

  • There is not too much to do in Zurich, so you only need a day or 2 here.
  • Everything is very expensive here as well so plan accordingly.
  • Zurich is a great destination to do day trips from. For example, we went to Lucerne which is an hour train, and it was like a fairyland.

Austria

Vienna

Description: This is a beautiful city filled with a lot of history. A great place to visit if you are looking to learn about European history but also have some fun.

Activities

  • Walking Tours! Soak up the city history
  • Museums

Food

  • Lugeck- amazing traditional Viennese food (get their truffle burger)
  • Palmenhaus- got lunch here, a beautiful butterfly garden

Where we stayed/how we got there:

  • Wombat’s City Hostel Vienna: great place to stay, clean and good location.
  • Flew by plane

Tips:

  • We only spent 24 hours here which was enough for most things we wanted to do.
  • Try their cuisine it is delicious.
  • Walk around and get lost in the streets and their beautiful architecture.

Hungary

Budapest

Description: Such an underrated city! We had so much fun here and it was very cheap and there were a ton of things to do. Amazing shopping and amazing food. We only spent 24 hours here as well but wish we had more time as there are so many things to explore in the city.

Activities

  • Site seeing- there is so many monumental buildings here.
  • Boat cruise at night
  • Museum of Terror
  • A lot of good shopping
  • Ferris Wheel in city center
  • Thermal baths: it seems gross, but it is a very cool experience

Food

  • Get a chimney cake.
  • Vinyl & Wood- got breakfast here and it was one of the best things I ate abroad.
  • Mazel tov- great Mediterranean
  • Salaam Bombay- unreal Indian food
  • There is amazing food so just explore
  • Simpla Kurtz- a really cool bar for going out

Where we stayed/how we got there:

  • Took bus here from Vienna- it was little sketchy, so I do not recommend.
  • Stayed in an Airbnb on the Pest side.

Tips:

  • Figure out the bus system here, it is so easy, convenient, and cheap.
  • Budapest can seem a little scary at first, but it is such an amazing city.

Portugal

Lisbon

Description: Lisbon is such a unique city with cobblestone roads and beautiful architecture. It is so lively and right near the beach. A great city to visit when it starts to get warm out.

Activities

  • Surfing! We booked surfing lessons, and it was the highlight of our trip. 10/10 recommend.
  • Tuk tuk tour around the city
  • Shopping
  • Day trip to Sintra
  • Visit the famous Pink Street

Food

  • Time Out Market-food Hall with a bunch of different types of food
  • Oak berry- yummy acai
  • Leonetta- good Italian food
  • Coya Taco
  • Pensao Amor- very cool bar

Where we stayed/how we got there:

  • Stayed in an AirBnb.
  • Flew by plane.

Tips:

  • Do the surfing! It was cheap and such a unique experience.
  • Explore the streets and all their little shops.
  • Ubers are very cheap here so take advantage.
  • Bring the right shoes the streets are steep and slippery.

Italy

Florence

Description: One of the most famous cities in Italy and it is for a reason. There are so many great places to eat, shops to explore, famous museums to see, and so much more. Really embrace the architecture and beauty that is Florence. The city is old fashioned but beautiful and fille with the nicest people.

Activities

  • Climb the Duomo
  • Boboli Gardens
  • Palace of Pitti
  • Visit the Ponte Vecchio
  • See the statue of David.
  • Visit the leather markets.
  • Visit Piazza Michelangelo at sunset.
  • Pasta/pizza making classes.
  • Other amazing museums

Food

  • Osteria delle tre panche
  • Acqua Al Due
  • Trattoria Za Za
  • Mamma Ginas
  • Misterpizza
  • The Central Market: a large food hall
  • Neruomo
  • Bucca Mario
  • Il profeta
  • La Giostra
  • Gusta Pizza
  • All Antico Vinaio
  • Antico Noe
  • Pino's
  • Shake Café
  • La menagerie
  • La Vespe
  • Melaleuca

Where we stayed/how we got there:

  • This was my home base, so we were in an apartment.

Tips:

  • If you are flying out of Florence look to fly out of Pisa or Bologna instead, it is way cheaper.
  • To find good gelato then need to either be in deep tins or just have no huge mounds
  • Don’t fall for the tourist traps, do your research!
